"It is not a trivial task to describe OptimalJ in a single Phrase.  It is perhaps best described as a supra-development environment that is model-driven and pattern-based.  The difficulty arises because OptimalJ provides facilities to automate and simplify the development of J2EE-based applications that extend beyond the boundaries of conventional integrated development environments (IDEs).  Indeed, OptimalJ includes an IDE (though you can use third party alternatives) as just a single part of its overall environment.  Alternatively, you could describe OptimalJ simply as a development accelerator, simply on the basis that it speeds up the development of J2EE-based applications. 

 

To be more specific, OptimalJ offers a model-driven, pattern-based environment that, on the one hand, separates business logic from all architectural considerations while, on the other, automatically generates the relevant structures for the deployment of n-tier applications.  In addition, an integration model separates integration needs from the platform.

 

Perhaps the most important advantage of the OptimalJ approach is that it simplifies Java development, enabling a team of architects, designers and developers to be more productive.  Compuware now offers specific versions of the product for each of the groups of individuals."

Bloor Research, 2003
